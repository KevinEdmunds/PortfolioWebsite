Weekly Dev Blog Section

  • Week 1

    This is a description

    Setting up GitHub: I have set up and briefly used GitHub prior to this course. I have taken the opportunity to review the website, as after creating an account, I have made use of GitHub desktop on a more frequent basis than the actual website. I noticed some beta features on the website, that are not present on the desktop software such as a “trending repositories” and a “recommended for you” section, which struck me as particularly interesting, because to me these seem like features on a social media platform, rather than a website that is used more so in a working/studying context.

    Readings: “As we may think” is a piece that is set more in reality, touching on the development of technology, primarily the evolution of technology responsible for creating and storing information. It speaks of the “memex”, a tool that closely mirrors some functionality that the internet also has (mainly that it served as a catalogue for pre-existing information). I think that the internet is becoming steadily more essential in our daily lives, as the retrieval of information is necessary for many of our daily tasks. It is also worth noting, however, that the longer the internet stays relevant, the more information is stockpiled in the same place. This can be seen as both a good thing and a bad thing. The collection and retrieval of information in one place can be seen as quite convenient. In contrast, with the amount of information on the internet, the reliability thereof becomes difficult to gauge.

    “The Library of Babel” was a fictional metaphorical piece, which I interpreted as a warning of overabundance of information (like the internet is today). I think that it is true that we are oversaturated with information – similar to the previous reading. Perhaps it is part of our responsibility as future web developers to present information to people in a way that is not overwhelming?

    Goals: One of my goals for this course is to try to work consistently through the course material. I would also like to further my technical ability in coding, in order to make functional websites, and understand the theoretical elements that make websites appealing, ultimately combining the two. I'm also interested in learning what our responsibilities are regarding what we put out onto the internet.
