  • Colour Theory

    This is some extra research I did to get a better understanding of colour theory and how I can use it effectively in my website

    Colour theory is an important aspect to consider with the development of my website. We can define colour theory as the interaction of colours within our website, through contrast, vibrancy and complementation (Tutsplus, 2010). Contrast refers to how easily the user can see things of different colours when they are next to each other. Vibrancy is important when considering how we want the user to feel. Bright colours are used to create energy, which is useful for trying to direct the user to navigate the page, and interact with elements. Darker colours are used to invoke calmness and focus. Complementation refers to the relationship that colours have with each other. When colours are on opposite ends of the colour wheel, they can create visual interest for the viewer to notice.

    Colour schemes/palettes are created by pairing a combination of colours together. There are 5 types of colour schemes thatcan be considered to make palettes:

    • Monochromic - This makes use of different shades, tints and tones of the same colour. This creates a harmonious look, but run the risk of becoming visually uninteresting eventually

    • Analogous - Colours that are adjacent to each other on the colour wheel are used. This has similar effects to a monochromatic colour palette, with a lesser risk of becoming monotonous

    • Complementary - colours that are opposite on the colour wheel are used. Because the colours are so different, their combination can be impactful and noticable. It is often the case that one of the colours is used primarily, while the second colour is used to accent or bring attention to certain areas.

    • Triadic - Three colours that are equidistant on the colour wheel are used. This is often used to create a vibrant, exciting look

    • Tetradic - Four colours that are equidistant on the colour wheel are used. Or two sets of complimentary colours can be used. This type of palette can offer a wide range of colour combinarions, and can create a balanced look when used effectively.

    I can make use of visual contrast, through the use of complimentary colours to make the text on my websites easy to read, and to communicate important feedback to my users. I will make use of a tetradic colour palette, because this way, I can make my background and foreground interact in visually interesting ways, and I can make text stand out against the background. I will make use of a dark background, with a light, dull foreground in order to draw the users attention to sections of writing in my page.
