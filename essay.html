Final Assessment - Using the framework of Ethical internet to analyse the Statement on AI Risk

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is rapidly changing the way that people use the internet. Made possible because of the internet, it continues to be integrated in our everyday lives. It has the potential to revolutionize aspects of our lives, such as automating tedious tasks providing personalized experiences for individuals and detecting fraud. As AI continues to permeate, we need to consider the implications that AI usage may bring. This essay aims to analyse the Statement on AI Risk, published by the Center for Artificial Intelligence, and discuss the risks of AI on the internet. By examining the statement and considering how AI is being used on the internet currently, we can gain valuable insight into the risks that are posed by this technology. Subsequently, we will use a framework of ethical internet principles to suggest some guidelines that can be applied to ensure the responsible and accountable use of AI on the internet. This essay advocates for the use of ethical principles to mitigate potential risks for AI, fostering responsible development and integration of the technology into society.

In May 2023, the Statement on AI Risk was published online on the Center for Artificial Intelligence (CAIS). The primary mission of the CAIS is to ensure that AI is developed and deployed in a safe manner, as the organisation aims to reduce the societal scale risks from AI through research. According to the statement “mitigating the risk of extinction from AI should be a global priority alongside other societal-scale risks such as pandemics and nuclear war” (CAIS, 2023). While this manifesto is not the first calling for the halting of AI development, it one of significance because it has been signed by over 350 AI researchers, including several significant AI experts and other notable figures (Paul, 2023). These individuals have come together under the CAIS and endorsed the nonprofit organization’s message. Their collective agreement amplifies the importance of addressing the risks associated with AI. Experts describe these risks as a “creeping deterioration”, opposed to a short-term catastrophe. The manifesto underscores the risks, which are that AI systems can be manipulated to do harmful things, and that more harm will arise when inaccurate, morally compromised AI systems become more integrated into domains of society (Paul, 2023). Notably, AI could potentially destabilize the internet through biased information, user manipulation, and job displacement. These risks pose challenges not only to the integrity of information sourced from the internet, but also the trust that society puts in this technology in the first place.

The term “artificial intelligence” is traditionally defined as an artificial creation of intelligence, that has the ability to learn, perceive or process natural language. Most AI that exists today can be described as “narrow”, which means that it is designed with the goal of performing a particular task within a domain (Internet Society, 2017). One of the key factors behind the advancement of AI is the process of machine learning. Machine learning is the process that enables a system to independently (without explicit programming) comprehend data and improve using neural networks and deep learning, by feeding it large amounts of learning data (Anon., 2021). This is a powerful tool that has allowed AI systems to achieve impressive results in a number of different fields. AI models are becoming increasingly capable, as many are capable of producing images, video and text that is indistinguishable from content created by people. For consumers, this could mean greater productivity, and lead to cheaper products as AI improves and continues to streamline the manufacturing process. Furthermore, new applications of AI have the possibility to create exciting opportunities for more effective medical care (Changwu Huang, 2022). In industries, AI can be used to detect and predict areas of potential hazard. The overall impact of these advancements can contribute to boosting productivity, which can benefit industries and society as a whole.

While there are benefits to using AI, it also poses some unavoidable risks that cannot be overlooked. It is true that AI technology holds potential for benefitting society in the future, the development thereof needs to be monitored so that risks can be mitigated. One such risk is an AI system’s ability to perpetuate bias. AI gets fed large amounts of learning data, such as web pages, articles and books, so that it can learn the patterns and connections between words (Anon., 2023). The data that an AI system is exposed to impacts the subject matter that it learns and makes connections between. This means that biased information can be perpetuated. Unfair discrimination may occur when biased representations are used in the training data that is used to train AI. This has the potential to cause unfair, discriminatory, and unethical predictions towards certain groups which are underrepresented in training data. Examples of this include AI that is built on racial biases, which have been used to predict future criminals (Julia Angwin, 2016), and AI that was used as a recruiting tool that had gender biases against women applicants (Dastin, 2018). The bias that AI inherits can create unexpected social bias and discrimination. Training data, the values of the developers creating the AI, the users who are using it, and the learning that take place through the usage of AI systems can all contribute to bias exhibited by an AI system.

It is also possible for individuals and organisations to misuse AI algorithms and technology for ill purposes. Some AI technologies are more effective than people at particular jobs, such as facial recognition and image generation (Changwu Huang, 2022). AI can be used to create fake images and videos that are believable to the human eye, which can be used to potentially spread misinformation. AI can be abused in ways that deviate it from its original intended purpose. Additionally, as AI continues to evolve, the differences between what work belonging to an AI and work belonging to an individual will decrease. Debates about ownership will need to take place, as it is unclear whether AI will have property rights or be considered as a “legal subject”.

The manner in which machine learning occur means that the decision-making process of AI may be difficult to understand, explain or predict. This has been seen in instances where AI-controlled cars, which should reduce traffic accidents are faced with a situation where it has to choose the lesser of two evils, such as injuring pedestrians, or sacrificing the passengers of the car (Changwu Huang, 2022). In cases such as this, AI systems have been found to be unpredictable. This raises questions about accountability, and intention of some AI systems.

The demand for the increase in AI on the internet could directly create for a greater demand of user data to be used for training AI models, to increase the performance of AI systems (Changwu Huang, 2022). This can encourage companies to collect or purchase user data, including sensitive personal information, at the risk of the user’s privacy. Data security issues have become increasingly common in the last few years as consequence of increased data collection.

The concept of Ethical Internet can be used to mitigate the risks of AI. An ethical internet promotes the responsible and accountable use of technology for the betterment of society. By embracing an ethical approach, a framework to guide the development and deployment of AI systems can be established. This will help the continuation of AI systems to be done in an ethical manner, preventing potential harms from occurring. AI system developers need to consider their collective responsibility in building AI systems, so that they do not pose risks to the internet or internet users (Internet Society, 2017). This requires that ethical considerations be taken into account to ensure the safe development and deployment of AI systems.

One crucial aspect that needs attention is the responsible considerations that can be put in place in order to improve the reliability and effectiveness of AI (Terry Yue Zhuo, 2023). Social stereotypes and biases can be avoided by ensuring that the training data used by AI systems is diverse and representational for all. Actively removing potential biases from the training data can further help to remove bias within an AI system (Terry Yue Zhuo, 2023). Furthermore, it is important to exercise due diligence and ensure the accuracy of training data, as well as eliminating inaccurate data from the training data.

Respecting fundamental human rights such as privacy is another important consideration in AI system development. AI systems should be developed in a manner that respects fundamental human rights, which include one’s privacy (Internet Society, 2017). Additionally, systems should be designed with safety and security in mind, to ensure that they protect user data in a responsible manner.

Transparency is necessary for AI systems. It is important that individuals should be able to understand how decisions are being made (Changwu Huang, 2022). Decisions made by an AI system should be possible to understand, especially in the case of the decisions that involve public safety or discriminatory practices in some form (Internet Society, 2017). Furthermore, accountability ensures that AI systems are developed and used in a responsible way. That way, any negative consequences that arise from AI system usage can be properly addressed.

The Statement on AI Risk published by the Center for Artificial Intelligence calls for urgent attention to the risks that AI presents, through highlighting the need for responsible development and mitigation of risks brought about by AI. While AI is a tool that continues to showcase how beneficial it can be, it also has some alarming flaws that need to be addressed. By scrutinising the risks that AI possess in the current climate, and advocating for ethical guidelines, we can make sure that AI continues to be integrated on the internet in a responsible manner. While AI continues to demonstrate its potential, we cannot overlook the risks and flaws that come with it. By addressing these risks now, we can ensure that AI systems in the future provide equitable and sustainable opportunities for all.

Case study - Department of Home Affairs website analysis - http://www.dha.gov.za/

The South African Department of Home Affairs website is supposed to be a resource utilized by South Africans, and internationals to gather information about civic services, and immigration technicalities. According to the site, it is “developed and maintained” by the Department of Home Affairs (Department of Home Affairs, 2023). This essay attempts to highlight the site's lack of usability, based on its UI and UX design decisions.

Jakob's Law states that users spend most of their time on other websites and prefer one's site to function similarly to other sites they are familiar with (Clark, 2018). Familiarity is valuable for users who are interacting with websites. It is useful for users to know how to interact with websites based on past experiences. The less effort the user needs to apply to figuring out the website, the more they can put into finding what they are looking for. This will help users to adapt to a webpage, with the experience of many websites before.

While Jakob's Law is applied to some key elements on the Department of Home Affairs website, it is disregarded for others. For example, while not immediately obvious, the home page features a search bar. Search bars are an important feature to have on large websites, as they help the user to navigate around the site with ease. This would be useful, except for the fact that it can only be found in the bottom right corner of the home page. Many popular large websites aim to have their search bars in either the top left or right of their pages (Wetherell, 2013). Placing it in an unusual place forces the user to look for it if they want to use it or give up and navigate without it. Additionally, the search bar can only be found on the home page of the site, meaning that users would have to navigate back to the home page first in order to make use of it. Due to the number of webpages on this site, it is clear to see that an effective search bar would be beneficial.

To make a website easy for a user to use, it is essential to have visual and structural consistency between web pages. Lessons that are learned with one system, are transferable to other systems (Norman, 2013). This can be seen as an extension of Jakob's Law.

One way in which this fails on the Department of Home Affairs website, is in the header. When hovering over certain header elements, one can notice that some of the navigation options that come up are fully capitalized, while others are not. There is seemingly no reason for this, as similar items do not necessarily function in the same way. Clicking “PAIA” in the “About” dropdown takes the user to a page on the Department of Home Affairs website, while clicking on “ACTS”, “BILLS” or “DRAFT BILLS” on the same dropdown, transfers the user to a different website entirely. Similarly, there is no way to tell whether clicking on a dropdown option will open a PDF in a new window or not.

Furthermore, some of the webpages are inconsistent with the navigation options that are provided on the page. Navigating to the “Contact” page from the header, the user is met with a page featuring a selection of links to visit some of the contact options in the dropdown. Navigating to the “Immigration” section provides the user with some information about the immigration services that Department of Home Affairs offers. Upon navigation to this page, however, the user will not be able to find links to any of the pages categorized in the dropdown menu for immigration services.

Using some parts of the Department of Home Affairs sets up expectations for how the rest of the site will work. When these expectations are not met, the user may have an unpleasant time trying to access information on the site (Mailloux, 1983). Inconsistencies can misdirect the user into thinking that a website will function in a particular way.

Hick's Law is used by designers to help to decrease number of options that a user is faced with at a time. The law states that the number and complexity of choices, presented to the user, is directly proportional to the amount of time that it takes the user to make a decision (Goldberg, 2023).

Hick's Law is not taken into consideration in the Department of Home Affairs website. In the header, which is used for navigation throughout the site, hovering over the “Immigration” option presents the user with a list of 32 different options. This section is the worst, but many of the other header options are not much better. This is visually overwhelming and difficult to navigate. It provides the user with too many options at once, which slows the user down from achieving their end goal. Additionally, there seems to be no logical order in which the list of options is presented to the viewer. To add to this, along the sides of the site, there are additional navigation buttons. This will only add to the user's confusion, as some of the buttons provide navigation to areas that cannot be accessed from the header.

As developers, part of our responsibility includes assisting the user to make decisions to reach their end goal. By following Hick's Law, we are able to reduce the cognitive load that a user may experience when faced with a number of decisions. Providing the user with too many options can slow them down, making them frustrating and diverting them from their objectives. The Department of Home Affairs website fails to give users the opportunity to make decisions in an effective way.

When we are considering websites, it is important not to overwhelm the user with too much information right away (UXPin, 2019). A website's home page should provide the user with information about what the site is able to offer to them, and how they are able to use it. It should be used to outline the type of content that the user should expect.

Entering the Department of Home Affairs website's home page should set the tone for the rest of the website. Due to the abundance of colours and confusing layout of information, this sets the impression that the website will be difficult to navigate. The majority of South Africans access the internet through mobile devices. Research has shown that in 2022, mobile devices accounted for as much as 77% of all internet usage across the country (Wondernet Express, 2021).

Having the knowledge that a large percentage of South Africans depend on mobile devices for internet access, a website that is mobile friendly should be a necessity.
